New Zealand

New Zealand, known as the Black Ferns, are the most successful team in Women's Rugby World Cup history, having won the tournament six times. Renowned for their speed, skill, and attacking flair, the Black Ferns are a dominant force in women's rugby. The team is celebrated for its strong Maori heritage and the haka performed before matches, which inspires both players and fans.

New Zealand's squad is packed with world-class talent, including several players who have been named World Rugby Women's Player of the Year. Their tactical awareness, physicality, and teamwork make them favorites in every tournament they enter. The Black Ferns' legacy is built on a culture of excellence and a commitment to growing the women's game both at home and internationally.
